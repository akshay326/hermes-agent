## What We Got Wrong

### A6: Habit Loop (cue-routine-reward)
The Duhigg model is an oversimplification. Implementation intentions (if-then plans) have far stronger evidence:
- Silva 2018 meta-analysis: d=0.36 effect size across 22 RCTs
- Ma 2023 meta-analysis: habit formation interventions significantly increase PA habit strength
- Routine-based cue planning > time-based (Keller 2021 RCT)
- Habit takes 59-70 days median, not 21 (Singh 2024 meta-analysis, range 18-254 days)

### A1: CBT Triangle Weighting
We overweighted cognitive distortion identification. Research says:
- Behavioral activation tasks completed at higher rates than cognitive restructuring (Furukawa 2018)
- Behavioral activation was primary driver of symptom reduction (Imai 2020)
- Additional benefit of cognitive restructuring in self-guided was limited (Steinberg 2024)
- In self-guided digital CBT, behavioral components are more accessible (Denecke 2022)

## What We Got Right
- Daily frequency (Heron & Smyth 2010, Bruijniks 2020)
- Biometric + CBT synergy (Ito-Masui 2023, Browne 2021)
- Self-monitoring drives engagement (Lee 2018)

## Rebuilt Architecture (v2)
1. Lead with behavioral activation, not cognitive restructuring
2. Implementation intentions (if-then plans) replace vague goals
3. Routine-based cue stacking > time-based (Keller 2021)
4. Adaptive feedback based on engagement level (Spring 2024 JAMA)
5. Non-judgmental tone enforced (Martinelli 2020, SDT)
6. Consistency tracking > intensity tracking (Bürgler 2026)
7. 59-70 day habit window — design for long haul (Singh 2024)
