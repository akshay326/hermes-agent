# Research-Backed Self-Esteem & Family Stress Interventions

Core domains to cover:
1. Self-esteem (CBT, self-compassion, behavioral activation)
2. Perfectionism (Frost Multidimensional Perfectionism Scale research, adaptive vs maladaptive)
3. Family systems (emotional cutoff, differentiation of self)
4. Financial stress & mental health (stress-process model)

## Key Researchers & Frameworks

### Self-Esteem
- **Mark Leary** — Sociometer Theory (self-esteem as social belonging indicator)
- **Kristin Neff** — Self-Compassion (vs self-esteem: kind self-judgment)
- **Nathaniel Branden** — The Six Pillars of Self-Esteem (competence, self-acceptance)

### Perfectionism
- **Frost et al.** — Multidimensional Perfectionism Scale (concern over mistakes, parental expectations)
- **Paul Hewitt & Gordon Flett** — Socially Prescribed Perfectionism (perceived others' expectations)
- **Thomas Curran & Andrew Hill** — "Perfectionism Is Increasing Over Time" (2019 meta-analysis)

### South Asian Family Dynamics
- **Majid & Ananda** — Enmeshment in collectivist cultures (boundary issues)
- **Berry & Song** — Emotional regulation in intergenerational families

### Financial Stress
- **Conger et al.** — Family Stress Model (economic pressure → emotional distress → family conflict)
- **Turley** — Financial strain and parental mental health

## Evidence-Based Interventions

### For Low Self-Esteem
1. **Behavioral experiments** — Test negative beliefs (Leary's sociometer theory suggests action > affirmation)
2. **Self-compassion breaks** — Neff's 3 components: mindfulness, common humanity, self-kindness
3. **Competence building** — Small mastery experiences (Bandura's self-efficacy theory)

### For Perfectionism
1. **Cognitive restructuring** — Challenge "all-or-nothing" thinking
2. **Exposure to imperfection** — Intentionally make small mistakes
3. **Compassionate self-talk** — Replace harsh inner critic

### For Family Financial Stress
1. **Communication skills** — Active listening, "I" statements
2. **Boundary setting** — Differentiation of self (Bowen theory)
3. **Shared problem-solving** — Involve family in solutions vs carrying alone

## Research Entry Points
